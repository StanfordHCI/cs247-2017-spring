Project 1: Crit

CS 247 takes many of the concepts you learned in introductory design coursework and deepens them. You are no longer an amateur, so we won't be treating you like one. We look forward to seeing you leap over our already-high expectations.

Goal

The goal of P1 is to introduce you to critique as a component of how design is evaluated and improved.

Equip yourself

A good drawing pad or sketchbook is a critical part of the designer's toolkit. Make sure you've purchased a pad and pen and bring it to class on Friday. Act quickly, because we are not the only class on campus that's asking you to buy a sketchbook and they do sell out. Important features: you must be able to easily tear out the pages (so we can tape them on the walls), no lines, no flimsy pages, no tiny pages (we recommend at least 5"x8"), no thin-lined pens (go for something with a reasonably thick ink stream), and no pencils. The Stanford Bookstore sells drawing pads, as does University Art and Accent Arts. If you get ahead of the game, use Amazon Prime Student to overnight some great ones from there.

Details

You will author two critiques for this assignment. For the first critique, pick an interface that someone else created: an app that you're interested in, a web interface, or an interactive physical product. For the second critique, pick an interface that you've created, perhaps in CS 147.

Critique each interface. Each critique should be no more than 300 words. Feel free to use a combination of text and visuals. Remember that a critique is "deeper" than a usability inspection. Please begin each critique with a full page screengrab of the interface so that we can see it clearly.

Finally, write up one paragraph answering the following questions:

  • Is your assessment subjective?
  • Do you think the subjectivity invalidates your assessment?
